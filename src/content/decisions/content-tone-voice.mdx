## Voice Principles

We defined three core voice principles:

### 1. Confident but not arrogant

**Do:** "Here's how to solve this problem."
**Don't:** "Obviously, you should do this."

We know our stuff and share it directly, but we don't talk down to people or assume they should already know things.

### 2. Helpful but not patronizing

**Do:** "If you're new to this, start here."
**Don't:** "Don't worry, this is really simple!"

We guide people without making them feel stupid. We acknowledge complexity while making it approachable.

### 3. Human but not unprofessional

**Do:** "We messed up, and here's how we're fixing it."
**Don't:** "Oopsie! Our bad!"

We can be warm and even occasionally funny, but we're still a business people trust with important work.

## Channel Adaptations

The voice stays consistent, but tone adapts:

- **Marketing**: More energetic, benefit-focused
- **Product UI**: Concise, action-oriented
- **Documentation**: Thorough, instructional
- **Support**: Empathetic, solution-focused
- **Social**: Conversational, engaging

## Writing Guidelines

Specific rules we established:

- Use "you" and "we" (not "users" or "the company")
- Active voice over passive
- Short sentences, short paragraphs
- Explain jargon or avoid it
- Lead with the benefit, not the feature

## Rollout Process

1. Created voice guidelines document with examples
2. Ran workshops with each content-creating team
3. Established peer review process
4. Built a "voice check" into content approval
5. Quarterly audits to measure consistency

## Measuring Success

We track voice consistency through:

- Quarterly content audits (random sample review)
- Customer feedback analysis (sentiment, word frequency)
- Internal surveys (do teams feel confident about voice?)
